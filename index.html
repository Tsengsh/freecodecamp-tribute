Frida Kahlo

Mexican feminist painter

Frida Kahlo is remembered for her self-portraits, pain and passion, and bold vibrant colors.

⌄

Timeline of Frida's Life




  • 1907

    BORN

    Frieda Kahlo Calderon is born in Coyoacan, a suburb of Mexico City.

  • 1922

    ENTER COLLEGE

    Frida Kahlo attends the Escuela Nacional Preparatoria with a view to studying medicine at university.

  • 1925

    TRAFFIC ACCIDENT

    Frida Kahlo is seriously injured in a collision between a tram and the bus. She spends a month in the Red Cross Hospital and starts painting during her convalescence.

  • 1929

    MARRIED DIEGO RIVERA

    Frida Kahlo and Diego Rivera are married. The couple live first in an apartment in the centre of Mexico City.

  • 1932

    MOVE TO DETROIT

    In April the couple move to Detroit, where Rivera has been awarded another commission.

  • 1933

    MOVE TO NEW YORK

    Frida Kahlo and Diego Rivera moved to New York, where Rivera paints a mural in Rockefeller Center.

  • 1933

    RETURNED MEXICO

    Frida Kahlo and Diego Rivera return to Mexico at the end of the year and move into a new house in San Angel.

  • 1938

    FIRST SOLO EXHIBITION

    he first solo exhibition of Frida Kahlo's works is held in October/November in Julien Levy's gallery in New York.

  • 1939

    TRAVELLED TO PARIS

    Frida travels to Paris, where she exhibits her works in the Renou & Colle gallery in March and meets the Surrealist painters.

  • 1940

    EMARRIED WITH DIEGO

    On 8 December, Frida Kahlo and Diego Rivera are married for a second time.

  • 1943

    PROFESSOR AT LA ESMERALDA SCHOOL

    She is awarded a professorship at the "La Esmeralda" school of Art.

  • 1948

    REJOINS THE MEXICAN COMMUNIST PARTY

    Frida Kahlo rejoins the Mexican Communist Party.

  • 1951

    SUPPORTED MEXICO'S PEACE MOVEMENT

    She helps to collect a list of signatures supporting the peace movement.

  • 1953

    ART EXHIBITION BY LOLA ALVAREZ BRAVO

    Lola Alvarez Bravo organizes the first solo exhibition of Frida Kahlo's works in Mexico in her gallery.

  • 1954

    PASSED AWAY

    She catches pneumonia, but while still convalescing she takes part in a demonstration against North-American intervention in Guatermala. On 13 July Frida Kahlo dies in the Blue House.
